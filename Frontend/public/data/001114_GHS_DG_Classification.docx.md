**4\) Rationale Summary – GHS & Dangerous Goods Determination**

**GHS Rationale**

* Ethanol has a **very low flash point (9.7 °C)** and produces flammable vapors capable of forming **explosive air–vapor mixtures**.

* These properties clearly meet the criteria for **Flammable Liquid Category 2** under GHS.

* No additional health or environmental hazard classes are assigned at this concentration (pure ethanol), but **flammability alone is sufficient** for GHS hazard classification. 

001114

**Dangerous Goods Rationale**

* Under UN Model Regulations and modal transport rules, liquids with a flash point below 23 °C and initial boiling point above 35 °C fall under **Class 3 – Flammable Liquids**.

* Ethanol meets these criteria and is therefore assigned:

  * **UN 1170**

  * **Class 3**

  * **Packing Group II** (medium danger)

* The classification is **consistent and mandatory across road, sea, and air transport**.
